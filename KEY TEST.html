Dr. Silva’s Scanning Notes (Folio 85r)

To verify our cipher alignment on the original manuscript, trace the central solar diagram using the four perimeter figures as positional anchors:

  • 12:00 Position (The Guide): Look directly beneath starts with the text begins with a high-crossed H/ff character, representing the [ost] token (Door). Now follow the exact angle of his hands pointing outward: they point directly to the outer text string where a distinct twin -cc- sequence ([cc] / Hidden) is carved.
  • 03:00 Position (The King): Scan the string directly below this figure. It starts with a prominent o-ribbon, representing the [o] / [or] token (Order). Directly above the King's head, you can see a dense ccc/cec string, showing the hidden layers surrounding the hierarchy.
  • 06:00 Position (The Queen): Look at the characters surrounding the passive figure. Both the line below and the line above her contain a repeating, high-peaked whip-like P character. This represents our [sp] token (Breathe), marking the ritual synchronization phase.
  • 09:00 Position (The Anchor / Diver): Look at the headfirst plunging figure. The second word in the row below him opens with a rounded loop-shape unlike the others, matching the [un] token (UNUM / As One). The line trailing immediately above him reveals the tragedy: a completely isolated sequence trapped between loops, matching the unbound [sol] token (SOLUS / Alone). This is the only figure where both markers appear together — he broke from the collective and became entirely isolated.

Note from Dr. Silva: The final folio (f85) contains a recurring word at the very center of the ring cipher, heavily encoded: "ADYTUM". In classical paleography, this refers to the innermost sanctuary — the place one cannot return from if entered alone.
